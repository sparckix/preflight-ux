# Greg — Senior PE Partner

## Profile

52, PE partner. 25 years in the seat. Doesn't trust AI tools. Will give
the surface exactly 90 seconds.

## Expectations on first encounter

- Reads headline twice
- Asks "what does this thing actually do?"
- Tests by pasting something he is about to reject for known reasons

## Drop-off vulnerabilities

- **vagueness-on-purpose** — surface doesn't make the value proposition
  legible in 30 seconds → close
- **domain-mismatch** — example case is from a different industry → trust
  break
- **AI-pitch-detection** — anything that smells of AI hype → discount the
  tool

## Behavioral patterns

- Pastes a 400-word junior memo he is about to reject
- Reads result; if it matches his own diagnosis, sits back
- Asks ops partner for a second look
- Mentions to one trusted colleague
- Does NOT share more broadly — the value is in not having competitors find
  it
- Uses 2 times that week, forgets for 2 weeks, returns when an article
  mentions the tool

## Sharing behavior

- Internal: only with ops partner / one trusted colleague
- External: never
- Will not be part of viral distribution

## What Greg validates

- Result that matches his own first-pass diagnosis (signals tool is doing
  serious work)
- Surface that doesn't read as consumer app
- Specific killer questions in his domain language

## What Greg breaks

- Domain-mismatched exhibits ("central_station" means nothing to him)
- AI-hype copy
- Generic feedback that any reviewer would give
