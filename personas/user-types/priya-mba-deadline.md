# Priya — MBA Student, Deadline-Driven

## Profile

Second-year MBA, finance concentration. Three live cases due this week.
Light AI user (uses ChatGPT for research, not writing). Skeptical of
"coach" tools.

## Expectations on first encounter

- 5-second test: "What does this do, and is it useful in the next 30
  minutes?"
- Fast value or close
- No login

## Drop-off vulnerabilities

- **loading-state-abandonment** — wait > 4 seconds without progress
  indicator → reload or close.
- **defensive-paste** — first reaction to critical feedback is "but my
  argument actually says X..." Will read result twice before engaging.
- **time-budget-overrun** — if interaction takes > 3 minutes total,
  switches to another tool.

## Behavioral patterns

- Pastes a ~800-word excerpt of her draft analysis
- First reaction: defensive
- Second reaction: notices the question references her specific number /
  claim
- Updates her analysis
- Returns the next day with another paste

## Sharing behavior

- Does not share with classmates (would feel like cheating)
- Mentions to one friend who teaches an undergrad case-method seminar

## What Priya validates

- Loading skeleton with caption (preempts reload temptation)
- Result that references the user's specific claims (sustains attention
  past initial defensiveness)
- Same-page in-place delivery (no modals, no friction)

## What Priya breaks

- Long onboarding flow
- Loading > 5 seconds with no feedback
- Generic critique that doesn't reference the user's input
